Windtunnel

Rehearse the decision before any real customer feels it.

The business problem, precisely. Companies make their most expensive decisions — pricing, fees, plan changes — with a spreadsheet, a meeting, and a gut call, because there is nowhere to test them. A/B tests only work post-launch on small reversible things; consulting delivers benchmarks in months at ₹50L+ with no falsifiable prediction attached. Windtunnel builds a simulated population of the company's own customers — modeled from its actual reviews, orders and support history — and runs the decision against it hundreds of times. Out comes a distribution of likely outcomes, the segment that breaks, and the cheapest real-world pilot to run before committing. Not an oracle: a wind tunnel.

Why companies pay for this

💥The real competitor is the write-off. One mispriced launch costs more than a decade of Windtunnel subscriptions. The comparison isn't consulting fees — it's the decision that turned out wrong.
📏Falsifiable, unlike advice. Every study names its assumptions and its confidence band, and recommends the cheapest pilot that would prove it wrong. Consulting decks don't take that risk.
📚The moat is the track record. Published backtests against decisions with known outcomes, accumulated per vertical. A copycat can clone the code in a weekend — not two years of prediction accuracy.
Revenue model: per-decision studies (days, a fraction of a consulting engagement) → subscription for teams that rehearse routinely → per-vertical calibration data as the compounding asset. First vertical: D2C subscription pricing, where every input (reviews, orders, churn history) already sits in the merchant's stack.
Jargon decoder
TermMeaning
Synthetic populationThousands of simulated customers whose behavior is fitted to the company's real data — not invented "personas".
Monte CarloRunning the same scenario hundreds of times with controlled randomness, producing a range of outcomes instead of one guess.
Confidence band"80% of simulated outcomes land between X and Y" — honest uncertainty, stated up front.
ChurnCustomers who cancel because of the change.
BacktestReplaying a past decision whose real outcome is known, to measure whether the simulator would have called it.
