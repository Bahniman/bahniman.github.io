Surety

The authorization and liability rail for AI agents.

The business problem, precisely. Enterprises want agents doing real work — renewing software, booking travel, executing procurement. Legal and compliance keep saying no, because today an agent with credentials is an unbounded power of attorney: no provable authorization, no limits that hold across tools, no record that survives a dispute. Surety is the missing control plane: the CFO signs a mandate (budget, permitted actions, approved vendors, an approval threshold), middleware enforces it on every single call, and a tamper-evident log turns "what did the agent do?" from a panic into a query. That log is also the endgame: with millions of certified actions on record, agent behavior becomes insurable — bonded agents, like bonded contractors.

Why enterprises pay for this

🔓Unblocks deployment. The #1 reason agent pilots die is that compliance can't sign off on unbounded action. A signed mandate is something a CFO can approve.
🧾Survives the audit. "Show me every action, who authorized it, and prove the log wasn't edited" — answered in one query instead of one bad week.
📉Becomes insurance. Certified action history is actuarial data. The endgame is bonding agents — premium priced from the log. That's the business no fast-follower can copy.
Revenue model: per-agent seat subscription for the enforcement layer → per-transaction fee on escrowed approvals → underwriting margin on agent bonds. Land with the audit log (single-player value on day one), expand to the mandate standard, end at insurance.
Jargon decoder
TermMeaning
AgentAn AI system that takes actions — spends, books, files — not just answers.
Mandate / delegation certificateA signed, scoped grant of authority: budget, permitted actions, vendors, threshold, expiry. Revocable any time.
Enforcement middlewareSoftware between the agent and its tools; every call is checked against the mandate before it executes.
EscrowAn above-threshold action pauses and waits for explicit human approval.
Hash-chained logEach log entry mathematically seals the previous one; editing any entry visibly breaks the chain there.
Bonded agentAn agent whose actions are insured, the way contractors are bonded — possible once behavior history is certified.
