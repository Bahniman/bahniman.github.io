Surety

A permission slip for your AI assistant — enforced, and provable.

What is this, in plain English? Imagine giving your assistant your credit card and a note: "up to ₹5,000, flights only, these two websites, ask me first above ₹2,500." Surety is that note, made digital and enforceable. The AI cannot exceed it, and every action is written into a record that cannot be quietly edited — like a diary where tearing out a page leaves visible damage.

Why this matters

Millions of AI assistants are about to hold people's delegated authority, and today there is no proof of who authorized what, no limits that travel with the agent, and no record that stands up afterward. Companies stall AI deployments at the pilot stage for exactly this reason. Surety is the missing permission-and-accountability layer — and at scale, its logs become the data an insurer needs to insure agent mistakes, the way contractors are bonded today.
