Heirloom

The firm's memory, owned by the firm — not by its AI vendor.

The business problem, precisely. Knowledge firms run on institutional memory — why we priced it that way, what this client hates, what we learned losing that pitch. Today that memory is (a) walking out the door with every exit, and (b) getting locked, team by team, into whichever AI tool each team adopted. That's a double write-off: a partner exit erases years of decision-rationale, and a vendor switch erases everything the tools had learned. Heirloom is the neutral memory layer the firm owns: every AI assistant reads it through one interface, each person sees only what their role allows, every answer carries its source, and the whole store exports in an open format. Employees leave, vendors change — the memory compounds instead of evaporating.

The three moments that destroy memory today

A partner resigns.
The firm switches AI vendors.
A memory turns out to be outdated.

The evidence

$47M / yrlost by the average large US business to inefficient knowledge sharing (Panopto/YouGov, n=1,001)
42%of institutional knowledge is unique to ONE person — no second copy exists when they leave (same study)
$31.5B / yrlost collectively by Fortune 500 firms from knowledge-sharing failure (IDC)
5.3 hrs / wkeach knowledge worker wastes waiting for, or recreating, knowledge that already exists
Why exit-time knowledge transfer fails: the giver has one foot out the door, the taker doesn't yet know what to ask, and neither is invested. The handover is a ritual, not a transfer. Memory has to be captured while the work happens — not extracted in the last two weeks. And the strategic tailwind is public: Satya Nadella has urged companies to build systems that retain institutional knowledge while staying free to swap the underlying AI models — to own their "learning loop" independent of any vendor (coverage). Portable, model-independent organizational memory is exactly that — sources: Panopto · IDC via Nuclino · HR Dive.

Why firms pay for this

🚪Exit insurance. A departing partner takes hundreds of decision-rationales with them. With Heirloom, the reasoning stays queryable — with sources — after the person is gone.
⏱️Ramp compression. New managers spend their first months re-learning what the firm already knew. A role-scoped memory makes month one look like month six.
🔓Vendor leverage. When memory exports cleanly, every AI contract renewal happens with a credible walk-away. CIOs pay for that alone.
Revenue model: per-seat subscription for the memory layer + migration services (moving a firm's existing knowledge in) + the export standard as the moat. The precedent is identity: when every app needed logins, the winner was the neutral layer they all integrated — not any app. Wedge market: consulting, law, agencies — where knowledge is the inventory and attrition is the write-off.
