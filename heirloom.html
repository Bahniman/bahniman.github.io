Heirloom

A company's memory, owned by the company — not by its AI vendor.

What is this, in plain English? Companies are pouring their decisions, meeting notes and hard-won lessons into AI tools — and each tool keeps that knowledge inside itself. Switch tools in three years, or lose the employee who knew "why we did it this way", and the memory is gone. Heirloom is a memory vault the company owns: every AI tool reads from it (only what each person is allowed to see), and the memory survives both resignations and vendor switches. Try it: ask the same question as three different employees.

Why this matters

When every app needed logins, the winner wasn't any app — it was the neutral identity layer they all plugged into (Okta). Memory is having that moment now. Companies signing multi-year AI contracts today aren't asking "can our knowledge leave with us?" — in three years they will ask it loudly, and the answer will be set by whoever built the neutral vault first. First customers: firms whose product is knowledge and whose people churn — consulting, law, agencies — where every resignation is currently a write-off.
