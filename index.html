I built the company’s eyesight

Product analytics, from scratch

Pendo was already paid for when I joined, but two or three people used it and only to float in-app surveys. Nobody used the analytics. I dug into it out of curiosity and found it could show what people actually did: click paths, funnel drop-offs, how often a feature got touched. So I built the usage-analytics function from nothing. What it surfaced was uncomfortable. Several genuinely good features were barely used, not because they were bad but because they were buried, or the setting had a name that meant nothing to the person reading it. Nobody reads release notes, so the product has to explain itself. I rewrote configuration names in plain language, added help text, and moved settings to where people would actually look, then used Pendo to prove usage climbed.

Built from scratch analytics function Was mid-rollout of hover descriptions when I left

Lost in literal translation

Localisation, owned the PRD end to end

Every new string was hard-coded and translated by pasting it into ChatGPT, by developers who did not speak the language. Clients who did speak it kept flagging translations that were technically correct and meaningless in context. I pushed my manager and my director to make it scalable rather than patch it string by string. They brought in a tech architect and shipped a central page to edit translations, which leadership read as done. It was not. The URL was per-client and hidden with no clear owner, strings sat in different files per module across a mixed PHP and Angular codebase, and finding the broken one was painful. I investigated it myself, documented what was actually wrong, and it became a roadmap item I owned the PRD for. Being in product meant I could fix it at the system level instead of one string at a time.
