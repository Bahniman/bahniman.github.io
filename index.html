The problem, in four verifiable numbers

₹96,000 cr1contractor dues reported in Maharashtra alone (2025)
₹1–3 lakh cr2estimated stuck nationally in delays & disputes
7–8 years3average construction arbitration; 85%+ claims pending
18–24%informal bridge rates contractors pay while they wait
The root cause is one document: the Measurement Book, the handwritten register on which every payment depends. CPWD already runs an electronic version on PFMS, paying ~₹20,000 crore a year digitally4 — and the money is still slow, because a digital record is still just a record. Realium changes what the measurement can do: makes it financeable, and makes the approval queue attributable.

Three layers, one rail

Layer 1 · Evidence

🛰️ Certify the facts

Geo-fenced site capture, AI quantity estimation, and a dual-key certificate — machine evidence plus an accountable engineer's signature — on a tamper-evident ledger.

Layer 2 · Authority

🛡️ Govern the actors

Every certifier and approver acts under a signed, scoped, revocable mandate. Every approval is a timestamped, signed event — so the queue itself becomes attributable.

Layer 3 · Liquidity

💸 Move the money

The verified e-invoice is financed: a bank advances 60% the next morning, holds 40% as buffer, and settles the balance when the treasury pays — contractor nets ~97% with day-1 cash.
