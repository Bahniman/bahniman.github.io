India's public works run on a handwritten register; contractors wait 6–18 months for money the state already owes them. We built the two missing rails as working software: GroundTruth turns physical work into a bank-financeable instrument the next morning, and Surety makes every actor in that chain — human or AI — operate under signed, enforceable, auditable authority.

18–24%informal bridge rates small contractors pay while waiting
The root cause is one document: the Measurement Book, the handwritten register in which an engineer records completed work and on which every payment depends. The government knows the direction — CPWD already runs an electronic measurement book paying ~₹20,000 crore a year digitally4 — but state departments remain on paper, and even the digitized register produces a record, not a financial instrument. Digitizing a document is not the transformation. Changing what the document can do is.

One platform, two rails

Rail 1 · certifies the facts

🛰️ GroundTruth

Physical work becomes a verified, bank-grade e-invoice: geo-fenced evidence, AI quantity estimation, an accountable engineer's dual-key signature, a tamper-evident ledger.

  • Bank advances 60% at T+1 against the instrument; 40% holdback is its buffer
  • Treasury settles later; balance released minus itemized charges — contractor nets ~97% with day-1 liquidity
  • The flywheel: clean settlement history raises the reliability score, which raises the advance rate (50% → 85%). Implemented, tested.

Rail 2 · governs the actors

🛡️ Surety

Every actor with authority in the chain — the certifying engineer, the approving officer, the AI agents doing the paperwork — operates under a signed, scoped, revocable mandate, enforced on every action.

  • Ed25519-signed mandates verify offline — no API, no shared secret
  • Category budgets, thresholds with human escrow, rate limits, kill switch
  • Hash-chained audit log: editing any entry visibly breaks the chain — court-grade history, and eventually actuarial data for insuring agent actions

Human–AI synergy is the architecture, not the theme. AI does what scales: observing sites, estimating quantities, enforcing policy on every call. Humans do what carries legal weight: authorizing mandates, attesting measurements, approving above thresholds. Neither can move money alone — that dual-key property is enforced in code on both rails.

Tech stack (all real, most of it already in the repos)

CaptureOffline-first Android capture with geotagged EXIF + device attestation; drone imagery where sanctioned under India's Drone Rules 2021. prototype: geo-fence check implemented & tested
VisionPhotogrammetry + segmentation-based quantity estimation against Schedule-of-Rates item classes, with calibrated confidence; cross-checks against open satellite (Sentinel-2) and OpenStreetMap basemaps. prototype: interface + confidence model; vision = roadmap
Trust coreEd25519 signatures (RFC 8032 — implemented pure-Python and validated against RFC test vectors), dual-key certificates binding evidence hashes, append-only hash-chained ledger. implemented, 47 tests across repos
PolicySurety engine: 10 ordered checks — signature, revocation, validity, active hours, tool→category map, domain allow-list, rate limit, category & total budgets, escrow threshold. Every decision ships its evidence list. implemented + threat model
FinanceAdvance-rate curve keyed to verification reliability; settlement waterfall with holdback-first deduction absorption and conservation checks; TReDS-pattern discounting as the bank-side model. implemented + financial model doc
IntegrationPFMS / e-MB adapter path (the rail CPWD already uses), bank APIs, and MCP for agent-side tooling — integration work, not research risk. roadmap, documented
Data layerPostgreSQL + PostGIS for geospatial state at scale (prototype uses SQLite by design — zero-dependency, runs anywhere in 10 seconds).

Why this wins on the judging criteria

Depth & honesty of problem.

A single named document as the point of failure, verifiable stuck-capital numbers, and the uncomfortable truth said plainly: discretion without evidence is where both delay and rent-seeking live.

DT centrality.

The operating model changes: what a measurement is (a financial instrument), who can act (mandated actors), when money moves (T+1). Remove the AI and the rails still function — AI makes them cheap, not possible.

Novelty.

Construction pitches are productivity tools; ours is the cash-flow ledger of the state plus the authority layer nobody builds. The advance-rate flywheel — verification history as a priceable credit asset — is the unobvious part, and it's implemented.

Feasibility & scale.

Every layer has a working Indian precedent: CPWD e-MB on PFMS, UPI, GST e-invoicing, TReDS, Drone Rules 2021. Pilot: one PWD division, six months, measurement-to-payment days 90→15. Scale: an open protocol, the UPI/ONDC playbook — then the same primitive prices insurance claims, agri lending and trade documents globally.
